Welcome To IIT Jodhpur's

Biosafety Website

Biological research often requires working with biological samples such as microbes, human / animal tissues and body fluids. Since microbes can be infectious and tissues / body fluids can carry these infectious agents, it is necessary to conduct research on these materials taking appropriate biosafety measures according to the risk posed by the agent in question. Moreover, genetic modification of living organisms can add to the risks they pose to individuals and the environment. The scope of this document is to introduce safe practices to conduct research using biological materials/ agents, hazardous organisms, genetically modified organisms, and living modified organisms at IIT Jodhpur in accordance with guidelines developed by the Department of Biotechnology, Govt. of India. The document will also introduce risk assessment and regulatory requirements to be fulfilled before initiating work.

Note: This is an introductory website. Please refer to Regulations and Guidelines on Biosafety of Recombinant DNA Research & Biocontainment, 2017 on https://ibkp.dbtindia.gov.in/Content/Rules for comprehensive biosafety guidelines as mandated by the Department of Biotechnology, Govt. of India.

Note: It is the responsibility of the Principal Investigator (PI) of any project to submit their proposed work to the Institutional Biosafety Committee (IBSC) in the prescribed format for its information, consideration and approval. For projects that require prior approval, work must not be initiated until approved by the IBSC or the competent authority.
For applications that fall within the scope of the IBSC, the IBSC will review the application and approve the application if found appropriate. Where the IBSC deems, the project requires further clearance from the Review Committee on Genetic Manipulation (RCGM) or other competent authorities, the IBSC shall forward the project along with its recommendations to the competent authority for final approval. If the PI determines that the work requires RCGM approval, please contact ibsc@iitj.ac.in to request for a sub user access to the IBKP portal, where the application should be submitted to the RCGM.
